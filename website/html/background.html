Background - rabies virus surveillance

Rabies is a devastating, invariably fatal zoonotic disease caused by a single stranded negative-sense RNA virus. Rabies is globally widespread and can infect and be transmitted by all mammal species, but the vast majority of human deaths worldwide result from bites by infected domestic dogs. Canine rabies elimination is feasible through sustained well-implemented mass dog vaccination programmes. A global initiative to end human deaths from dog-mediated rabies by 2030 is now underway and practical, effective surveillance tools are required to support this campaign.

Surveillance is critical to inform infectious disease management and control programmes and genomic data is emerging as a powerful new surveillance tool. Pathogen sequencing has become increasingly routine in well-resourced public health laboratories, but in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) where most emerging and endemic zoonoses occur, sequencing capacity is more limited and the direct application of genomics-informed surveillance is still at an early stage.
